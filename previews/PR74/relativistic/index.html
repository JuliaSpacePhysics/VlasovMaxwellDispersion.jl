Relativistic response and continuation

1 Overview

Dispersion-root tracing evaluates the response at damped frequencies, Im𝜔<0. This note derives the continuation used for relativistic, oblique, damped (superluminal) modes. The requested sheet is reached from the upper half-plane through the subluminal interval |Re𝜔|<|𝑘∥|. Its correction is a sum of residues integrated over transported open paths. Convergence of the harmonic sum imposes a separate accuracy limit.

2 Continuation of moving pole integrals

For a holomorphic numerator 𝑔,

𝜑(𝑝)=∫−𝑤𝑤𝑔(𝑥)𝑥−𝑝d𝑥

is holomorphic off the segment. Continuing the upper branch downward through the segment gives

𝜑cont(𝑝)=𝜑(𝑝)+2𝜋𝑖 𝑔(𝑝),Im𝑝<0.

Now let the pole and integration interval depend on 𝑦:

𝐼(𝜔)=∫d𝑦∫−𝑤(𝑦)𝑤(𝑦)𝑔(𝑥,𝑦)𝑥−𝑝(𝑦,𝜔)d𝑥.

On the real axis, define

Γ(𝜔)≔{𝑦:𝑝(𝑦,𝜔)2<𝑤(𝑦)2}.

Slice-wise boundary-value gives: 𝐼(𝜔±𝑖0)=p.v.𝐼(𝜔)±𝑖𝜋∫Γ(𝜔)𝑔(𝑝(𝑦,𝜔),𝑦)d𝑦. Hence the analytic continuation is

𝐼cont(𝜔)=𝐼(𝜔)+2𝜋𝑖∫Γ(𝜔)𝑔(𝑝(𝑦,𝜔),𝑦)d𝑦

Off-axis, Γ(𝜔) is transported holomorphically: its finite endpoints follow the roots 𝑝2=𝑤2, while unbounded ends remain in transported sectors. If 𝑔(𝑥,𝑦) is entire and decays at infinity, Cauchy’s theorem means that only endpoints and the connected sector 𝑆(𝜔) matter. Singular 𝑔 also requires continuation to record the winding.

3 General relativistic response

Set 𝑎=𝑘⟂/Ω0 (subscript 0 omitted for simplicity when clear from context), 𝑢=𝑝∥ and 𝑞=𝑝⟂, and write the distribution as 𝑓(𝛾,𝑢). With 𝑞2=𝛾2−1−𝑢2 and 𝑤(𝛾)=𝛾2−1, the harmonic response is

𝐹(𝜔)=∑𝑛=−∞∞𝐼𝑛(𝜔),𝐼𝑛=∫1∞d𝛾∫−𝑤(𝛾)𝑤(𝛾)d𝑢 𝒰︀𝒯𝑛𝐷𝑛,𝐷𝑛≔𝛾𝜔−𝑘∥𝑢−𝑛Ω0,

where 𝒰︀(𝛾,𝑢;𝜔)=𝜔𝜕𝛾𝑓+𝑘∥𝜕𝑢𝑓 and 𝒯𝑛(𝑎𝑞,𝑢,𝑞) is the harmonic Bessel tensor, bilinears in 𝑞𝐽𝑛±1(𝑎𝑞) and 𝑢𝐽𝑛(𝑎𝑞).

On resonance 𝐷𝑛(𝑢𝑛)=0,

𝑢𝑛(𝛾)=𝛾𝜔−𝑛Ω𝑘∥,𝑞𝑛2(𝛾)=𝛾2−1−𝑢𝑛2(𝛾).

The pole crosses downward for 𝑘∥>0 and upward for 𝑘∥<0. Combining the crossing orientation with the residue gives

Δ𝐼𝑛(𝜔)=−2𝜋𝑖|𝑘∥|∫Γ𝑛(𝜔)𝒰︀(𝛾,𝑢𝑛;𝜔)𝒯𝑛(𝑎𝑞𝑛,𝑢𝑛,𝑞𝑛)d𝛾,𝐹germ=𝐹+∑𝑛Δ𝐼𝑛.

3.1 Endpoint and contour

The contour starts at the entering root of 𝑞𝑛2=0:

𝛾0𝑛=𝑛2Ω2+𝑘∥2𝑛Ω𝜔+|𝑘∥|Δ𝑛=𝑛Ω𝜔−|𝑘∥|Δ𝑛𝜔2−𝑘∥2,Δ𝑛=𝑛2Ω2+𝑘∥2−𝜔2.

Let 𝜔=𝜔𝑟+𝑖𝜈. For 𝜈<0, the radicand 𝑛2Ω2+𝑘∥2−𝜔2 avoids the principal-square-root branch cut (−∞,0], so this is the branch continued from the real subluminal interval. At finite damping, the endpoint remains finite even while passing around the light-line singularity.

Parameterize contour tail of Γ𝑛 by 𝛾=𝛾0𝑛+𝑡𝑒𝑖𝜃. Asymptotically 𝑞𝑛∼𝑐𝛾 with 𝑐=1−𝜔2/𝑘∥2, 𝒯︀𝑛 (Bessel bilinears) grows as 𝑒2|Im(𝑎𝑞𝑛)| against the distribution decay 𝑒−𝜇Re𝛾, giving

|integrand|∼𝑒−𝑡𝑟(𝜃),where𝑟(𝜃)≔𝜇cos𝜃−2|𝑎‖𝑐‖sin(𝜃+arg𝑐)|.

Note that 𝑆(𝜔) always contains 𝜃∗=−arg𝑐, which eliminates Bessel growth and leaves 𝜇cos(arg𝑐)>0.

3.2 Summing continuations: Harmonic convergence

Each transported harmonic is finite; their sum need not converge. For 𝜔𝑟>𝑘∥>0 and 𝑛Ω<0, 𝛾0𝑛≈|𝑛Ω|/(𝑘∥−𝜔) has Re𝛾0𝑛<0, with magnitude linear in |𝑛|. Large-order Bessel decay offsets the distribution growth only when

2𝜂(𝑥)>𝜌(𝜔)⇔sum converges as|Δ𝐼𝑛|∼exp(|𝑛|(𝜌(𝜔)−2𝜂(𝑥)))𝜌(𝜔)=𝜇|Ω|(𝜔𝑟−𝑘∥)|𝑘∥−𝜔|2,𝜂(𝑥)=ln((1+1−𝑥2)/𝑥)−1−𝑥2𝑥=𝑘⟂|𝑐|/(|𝑘∥−𝜔|)

for 𝑥<1, the Bessel sub-turning-point exponent 𝜂=0 for 𝑥≥1. Inside this domain, harmonic shells decay to tolerance; outside, they decrease then grow. Termwise harmonic continuation may therefore diverge even when a continuation of the summed response exists (Paley–Wiener theorem).

4 Parametrize by 𝑝⟂

Although 𝐷𝑛 is not rational in 𝑢, its conjugate factor gives

𝐷𝑛𝐷𝑛̃=𝐴𝑢2+𝐵𝑢+𝐶,𝐷𝑛̃≔𝜔𝛾+𝑘∥𝑢+𝑛Ω,𝐴=𝜔2−𝑘∥2,𝐵=−2𝑘∥𝑛Ω,𝐶=𝜔2(1+𝑞2)−𝑛2Ω2.

Thus 1/𝐷𝑛=𝐷𝑛̃/[𝐴(𝑢−𝑢+)(𝑢−𝑢−)]. One root solves 𝐷𝑛=0; the other solves 𝐷𝑛̃=0 and has zero residue. Keeping both avoids branch-dependent root classification. The residue at either root is evaluated stably as

𝑟𝑠=𝑔(𝑢𝑠)𝐷𝑛̃(𝑢𝑠)2𝐴𝑢𝑠+𝐵

Its two rationalized roots coalesce at where its discriminant 𝐵2−4𝐴𝐶=4𝜔2(𝑛2Ω2+(𝑘∥2−𝜔2)(1+𝑞2)) vanishes,

𝑞apex2=𝑛2Ω2𝜔2−𝑘∥2−1,

the branch point lies below the path for 𝜈>0, touches it at 𝜈=0, and moves above it for 𝜈<0.

This continuation is exact for 𝜔𝑟2<𝑘∥2. There the discriminant does not vanish on the outer path, and the physical pole crosses uniformly. Finite support endpoints sit where 𝑓0≈0, so pole-endpoint collisions carry negligible coefficients.

For 𝜔𝑟2>𝑘∥2, inside the resonant band 𝑘∥2<𝜔𝑟2<𝑘∥2+𝑛2Ω2, this apex reaches the real 𝑞 path, and the straight real-sliced integral omits the cut discontinuity.

Comment: this formulation is more computationally efficient. The Bessel argument 𝑧=𝑘⟂𝑝⟂/Ω0 is independent of the inner 𝑝∥, so the Bessel tensor can be computed once per slice.

4.1 Why energy is the transport coordinate

In energy coordinates, 𝑢𝑛 and 𝑞𝑛2 are polynomial in 𝛾, and 𝒯𝑛 is even in 𝑞𝑛. For entire 𝑓(𝛾,𝑢), the integrand is entire in 𝛾 and 𝑞𝑛2=0 is a regular endpoint (the artificial singularity at the apex is just a regular interior point of the integrand).

Using 𝑞 as transport coordinate instead fuses physical 𝛾>0 and mirror 𝛾<0 roots at the apex. It also invites the wrong branch 1+𝑞𝑛2+𝑢𝑛2=𝛾2=±𝛾 when evaluating at complex momenta. The principal root fails once the transported endpoint enters ℜ(𝛾)<0.

5 Maxwell-Jüttner fast paths

For 𝑓=𝑒−𝜇𝛾 the Swanson gyrophase-time form 𝜒∝∫0∞𝑒−𝑅𝐾𝜈(𝑅)-termsd𝜉,

𝑅=(𝜇Ω−𝑖𝜉𝜔)2+2𝑘⟂2(1−cos𝜉)+(𝑘∥𝜉)2Ω2
  • Parallel (𝑘⟂=0): 𝑅0=𝐴𝐵/Ω2 factorizes; per-factor principal square roots are the germ branch along any path avoiding 𝑠∗∓=−𝑖𝜇|Ω|/(𝜔∓𝑘∥) (radial cuts). Integrate each folded harmonic on a ray in the wedge between the cut angles; the cyclotron-resonant harmonic may need a dogleg with sign flips at cut crossings. Certified to 10−8–10−10 against the corrected López closed form.
  • Oblique (𝑘⟂≠0): transported residue contour

6 Why oblique continuation changes representation

In the time-domain form 𝜒=∫0∞𝑒𝑖𝜔𝑡𝐶(𝑡)d𝑡, damping demands a contour tilted upward forever, while the gyration phase confines 𝐶 to a strip |Im𝑡|≲log(𝜇Ω/𝑘⟂)/Ω. No contour does both.

At 𝑘⟂≠0 the monodromy content of this sheet is an infinite reorganizing ladder. Every finite evaluator is exact where its representation converges, and an optimally-truncated surrogate with a quantifiable floor beyond.

7 Scope and implementation

Path Γ(𝜔) now requires an analytic complex energy-form gradient and an entire integrand. Exponential families satisfy these assumptions; kappa families need unimplemented singularity and winding tracking and extend the formulation to general energy forms.

This note describes the subluminal-germ sheet. It can be exponentially far from the physical boundary value near marginal in-band frequencies.
