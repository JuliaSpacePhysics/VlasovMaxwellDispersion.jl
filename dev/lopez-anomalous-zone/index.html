The "anomalous zone" of López et al. (2014) is an analytic-continuation artifact

Claim. The finite-𝜔𝑟 descending segment of the Alfvén/ion-cyclotron branch (“anomalous zone”, 𝜕𝜔𝑟/𝜕𝑘<0) published for relativistic Maxwell–Jüttner pair plasmas by López et al. 2014 (PoP 21, 092107, Fig. 1) and largely reproduced by ALPS (Verscharen et al. 2018, JPP 84, 905840403, Fig. 5) is not a plasma mode. In López’s calculation it is a zero of a non-holomorphic function: López’s closed-form continuation is continuous across the real-𝜔 axis but violates the Cauchy–Riemann equations below it. The unique analytic continuation instead carries the branch structure: the propagating root rises toward the light line while a separate purely imaginary (𝜔𝑟=0, aperiodic) family carries the strong damping.

Setup: electron–positron pair plasma, both species Maxwell–Jüttner with 𝜇=𝑚𝑐2/𝑇; 𝜔𝑝𝑒2/Ω𝑐2=1 per species; López normalization 𝑥=𝜔/Ω𝑐, 𝑦=𝑐𝑘/Ω𝑐, 𝑧=𝑥/𝑦, 𝑡=1/𝑦. VlasovMaxwellDispersion.jl (VMD) evaluates the factorized parallel L-mode (mode = :L at exactly 𝑘⟂=0) throughout. At the tested 𝜇=2 roots, VMD’s two independent relativistic evaluators (Swanson time-integral closed form; general momentum-grid CoupledVDF) agree to |Δ𝜔|≲6×10−4, including deep-damped points.

where the real support endpoints are

𝛾1𝑒(𝑅)=𝑅𝑡+𝑡2+1−𝑅21−𝑅2,𝛾1𝑝(𝑅)=−𝑅𝑡+𝑡2+1−𝑅21−𝑅2.

Tracing Λ𝐿=0 reproduces the published curves (script 02): the 𝜇=10 hump peaking at 𝜔𝑟=0.443 at 𝑘=1.7 (paper: 0.444 at 1.65), descending to 𝜔𝑟→0 by 𝑘≈2.4; and the 𝜇=2 hump peaking 𝜔𝑟≈0.125 near 𝑘≈0.47, descending to 0 by 𝑘≈0.6–0.65. Thus the implementation matches their formula; the disagreement with VMD is an artifact in their formula.

2 Non-holomorphy of the continued Λ𝐿: analytic diagnosis and measurement

Fix Im𝑧<0, so 𝜎=2, set 𝑢=Re𝑧, and let 𝐻𝑗(𝛾;𝑢) denote López’s piecewise Heaviside support. With 𝑦 fixed, the 𝜃 contribution to Eq. 26 is

𝐶𝜃(𝑧,𝑧̄)=𝑖𝜋𝜎𝜇24𝐾2(𝜇)𝑦4∑𝑗∫1∞𝑒−𝜇𝛾𝑎𝑗(𝛾,𝑧)𝐻𝑗(𝛾;𝑢)d𝛾,

where

𝑎𝑒,𝑝(𝛾,𝑧)=(𝑦2−𝑦2𝑧2)𝛾2∓2𝑦𝑧𝛾−(1+𝑦2)𝑧.

This includes both the 1𝑥 prefactor and the 𝑥, 𝑥2 terms in 𝑤𝑗; 𝑎𝑗 is holomorphic away from 𝑧=0. Only 𝐻𝑗 depends on 𝑧̄, through 𝑢=𝑧+𝑧̄2. Away from changes in support topology,

𝜕𝐶𝜃𝜕𝑧̄=𝑖𝜋𝜎𝜇28𝐾2(𝜇)𝑦4∑𝑗∫1∞𝑒−𝜇𝛾𝑎𝑗(𝛾,𝑧)𝜕𝐻𝑗(𝛾;𝑢)𝜕𝑢d𝛾.

The derivative of 𝐻𝑗 is a moving-endpoint boundary term. Below the axis, 𝜕Λ𝐿/𝜕𝑧̄ is nonzero, while both the 𝜃-less expression and VMD satisfy Cauchy–Riemann to finite-difference accuracy.

López imposes only the continuity condition limΓ→0+Λ+=limΓ→0−Λ− (their Eqs. 19–20). Continuity of boundary values is necessary but not sufficient for analytic continuation; the missing requirement is holomorphy in 𝑧. Moving support through (Re𝑧,sign Im𝑧) introduces the nonzero boundary terms.

4 The fix: an analytic continuation term (script 06)

The correct continuation keeps the resonant-support endpoint analytic in 𝑧 instead of freezing it at Re𝑧. For the subluminal window |Re𝑧|<1:

Λ𝐿corr(𝑧)=Λ𝐿𝜃-less(𝑧)+𝑖𝜋𝜎𝜇24𝐾2(𝜇)𝑥𝑦3∑𝑗∫𝛾1𝑗(𝑧)∞𝑒−𝜇𝛾𝑤𝑗(𝛾;𝑥,𝑦)d𝛾,𝛾1𝑒(𝑧)=𝑧𝑡+𝑡2+1−𝑧21−𝑧2,

𝛾1𝑝(𝑧) replaces 𝑧𝑡 by −𝑧𝑡. The tail integral runs along any complex path from the endpoint to the positive real ray; its integrand is entire. Here 𝜎=2 below the axis, while 𝜎=1 denotes the boundary value on the axis. Results:

  • on the axis it equals López to ≲3×10−10, preserving continuity condition;
  • holomorphy defect 10−6–10−7 in the lower half-plane (vs their 0.09–3.1; Figure 1 right panel);

Root polishing from the López descent seeds now converges to the VMD roots: 0.16297−0.11048𝑖 at 𝑘=0.5 and 0.20540−0.18512𝑖 at 𝑘=0.6, with |Λ|∼10−15. No corrected root remains at the descent locations.

4.1 Superluminal extension: following the branch across the light line (script 09)

The corrected term is not confined to the subluminal window. On the sheet reached by continuous continuation through the lower half-plane, its branch functions are analytic along paths avoiding 𝑧=±1 (the principal 𝑡2+1−𝑧2 cut needs real 𝑧2>1+𝑡2; the endpoint denominators 1−𝑧2 pole only at 𝑧=±1), so the same formula followed continuously through the gap 1<Re𝑧<1+𝑡2 — i.e. 𝑘𝑐<𝜔𝑟<𝑘2𝑐2+Ω2 — is the unique continuation of the subluminal germ.

The physics it reveals (𝜇=2): the A/IC branch crosses the light line at 𝑘≈1.9 and stays slightly superluminal (𝜔𝑟/𝑘→1.04 by 𝑘=3) with slowly recovering damping (𝛾=−0.484 at 𝑘=1.6, −0.451 at 3.0, −0.348 at 6.0). Relativistic cyclotron resonance survives 𝑣ph>𝑐: the resonant ellipse in momentum space persists while 𝜔𝑟2−𝑘2𝑐2≲Ω2, fading only as the resonant Lorentz factors climb into the 𝑒−𝜇𝛾 tail.

5 What the physical dispersion looks like

The anomaly-relevant fact is the separation of two roots. The propagating A/IC root reaches 𝛾≈−0.49, then rises through the light line; the aperiodic root remains on 𝜔𝑟=0 and carries the published damping dive. They never coalesce. At 𝜇=10 the aperiodic root lies below the published frame until 𝑘≈3.05.

6 Why ALPS partially reproduces the same artifact

ALPS does not evaluate López’s closed form, yet its Fig. 5 chases the same descent. The reason is that its damped-side machinery makes the same class of continuation through real-part criteria.

In their scheme: a pole contributes only when its real part is deemed inside the integration domain via −𝑃max,∥𝑗≤Re(𝑝̄pole)≤+𝑃max,∥𝑗 (their Eq. 3.18), with 𝑝̄pole=Γ𝜔/(𝑘∥𝑐)−𝑛Ω0𝑗/(𝑘∥𝑐) (their Eq. 3.15);

Their lower-half-plane term selects resonant support using Re𝑧. As in §2, their Wirtinger derivative contains moving-boundary terms; absent exact cancellation, this construction is not holomorphic below the axis.

7 Conclusions

The analysis establishes the defect in López’s closed form.

  1. The anomalous-zone 𝜔𝑟 descent of López et al. 2014 — and its Fig.-5 reproduction in Verscharen et al. 2018, whose fitted continuation partially chases the same artifact — is a zero of a non-holomorphic function, not a plasma mode.
  2. Continuing the support endpoint as a complex function of 𝑧 restores Cauchy–Riemann behavior and yields the VMD roots. AAA extrapolation from upper-half-plane data corroborates the same roots.
  3. In the computed 𝜇=2 and 𝜇=10 cases, the propagating root rises instead of descending to the imaginary axis. Separate aperiodic roots carry the strong damping seen in the published curves.

8 Appendix: The 𝑘→0 ladder

On the imaginary axis below the origin (𝜔=−𝑖|𝛾|, 𝜎=2) the corrected continuation of §4 reads

Λ𝐿=Λ𝐿𝜃-less+𝑖𝜋𝜎⋅𝜇24𝐾2(𝜇)𝑥𝑦3∑𝑗=𝑒,𝑝∫𝛾1𝑗(𝑧)∞𝑒−𝜇𝛾𝑤𝑗(𝛾)d𝛾,

with weights 𝑤𝑒,𝑝=(𝑦2−𝑥2)𝛾2∓2𝑥𝛾−(1+𝑦2) and the analytic support endpoints 𝛾1𝑒,1𝑝(𝑧)=(±𝑧𝑡+𝑡2+1−𝑧2)/(1−𝑧2). Take 𝑦→0 at fixed 𝑥 (so 𝑧=𝑥/𝑦→∞). Everything simplifies exactly:

  1. Endpoints. 𝛾1𝑒(𝑧)→−1/𝑥 and 𝛾1𝑝(𝑧)→+1/𝑥 (with an 𝑂(𝑦) real correction). These are precisely the analytic continuations of the 𝑘=0 resonant Lorentz factor: a particle resonates with the wave iff 𝛾𝜔=∓Ω, i.e. 𝛾res=∓Ω/𝜔.
  2. Weights. 𝑤𝑒,𝑝→−(𝑥𝛾±1)2.

  3. Tails. The tail integrals become elementary, ∫∓1/𝑥∞𝑒−𝜇𝛾(𝑥𝛾±1)2d𝛾=(2𝑥2/𝜇3)𝑒±𝜇/𝑥, so the whole continuation term collapses to

    𝐶=−𝑖2𝜋𝑥𝜇𝐾2(𝜇)𝑦3cosh(𝜇𝑥)⇒𝐶|𝑥=−𝑖|𝛾|=−2𝜋|𝛾|𝜇𝐾2(𝜇)𝑦3cos(𝜇Ω|𝛾|),

    manifestly real on the axis.

  4. Background. The 𝜃-less part stays 𝑂(1): expanding its finite-support 𝜉-integral in 𝑣/𝑤, the 𝑂(𝑡−1) moment cancels the −𝜇/𝑦2 cold term exactly (via ∫1∞𝑒−𝜇𝛾𝛾𝛾2−1d𝛾=𝐾2/𝜇), leaving 𝐷(|𝛾|)=1−(𝜇2/3𝐾2)∫1∞𝑒−𝜇𝛾𝛾(𝛾2−1)3/2/(|𝛾|2𝛾2+1)d𝛾.

Numerically, Λ𝐿(−𝑖|𝛾|,𝑦)=𝐷(|𝛾|)+𝐶 holds with relative error ∼𝑦 (checked at 𝑦=0.02→0.005, 𝜇=2 and 10; script 09). The 𝑦−3 term therefore dominates as 𝑘→0 and pins every axis zero to

cos(𝜇Ω|𝛾|)=0⇒𝛾𝑛=−2𝜇Ω𝜋(2𝑛−1).∎

The physics is transparent: continuing the resonance to an aperiodic 𝜔=−𝑖|𝛾| makes the resonant energy imaginary, 𝛾res=𝑖Ω/|𝛾|, so the Jüttner factor 𝑒−𝜇𝛾res becomes a pure phase 𝑒𝑖𝜇Ω/|𝛾|. Electron and positron contribute conjugate phases, and their interference — the cosine — vanishes at odd quarter-periods. The aperiodic ladder is an interference pattern of the analytically continued cyclotron resonance in the relativistic Boltzmann factor, with the odd harmonics fixed by the pair symmetry.

At finite 𝑘 the quantization survives as a stack of in-band quasimodes. The ladder members leave the imaginary axis pairwise as 𝑘 grows and become propagating damped modes hugging the light line from below — the “second family” is simply the least-damped member. At 𝑘=1.5, 𝜇=2 carries one (1.494−0.19𝑖), while 𝜇=10 carries at least five (1.482−0.24𝑖, 1.475−0.29𝑖, 1.434−0.45𝑖, 1.361−0.63𝑖, 1.051−1.05𝑖; each polished to |Λ|<10−9): the member count at fixed 𝑘 scales with 𝜇 like the 𝑘→0 ladder, because a larger 𝜇 means more accumulated resonance phase. Each member keeps nearly 𝑘-independent damping (𝜇=10 third member: 𝛾=−0.45±0.01 over 𝑘=0.6–1.4, descending to its axis pair below 𝑘≈0.4; least-damped member: −0.24→−0.27 over 𝑘=1.5–4.5; 𝜇=2: ≈−0.19). These are damped quasimodes of the relativistic cyclotron continuum — discrete interference zeros tied to the band where 𝛾𝐿(𝜔−𝑘∥𝑣∥)=±Ω is solvable — with no nonrelativistic counterpart. Their large-𝑘 fate depends on where the EM branch sits relative to the band edge: at 𝜇=2, 2Π2𝐾1/𝐾2=1.102≈Ω2, so the arriving quasimode finds a marginal real mode at the edge and lands on it (the 𝑘≈6.1 shutoff of §5); at 𝜇=10 the EM branch (1.716) is well separated, and the least-damped quasimode crosses the real-𝜔 band edge at finite depth (𝛾≈−0.28 near 𝑘≈5.8, the edge broadened by its own damping) and persists as a distinct damped mode (traced to 𝑘=9, 𝜔𝑟−𝑘→0.16, 𝛾→−0.31).

Corollary: the non-holomorphic continuation misses the ladder entirely. López’s 𝜃 term freezes the support at 𝛾1(Re𝑧=0)≈𝑡2+1≈1/𝑦, so their continuation term is 𝑂(𝑒−𝜇/𝑦) — invisible. Checked at 𝑘=0.01, 𝜇=2: their Λ𝐿 is smooth and positive across 𝑠=4/𝜋 (1.47) while the correct function swings through zero from +3×106 to −3×106. The entire aperiodic sector — the roots that carry the published damping dive — does not exist in their closed form at small 𝑘; it is recovered only by the holomorphic continuation.
